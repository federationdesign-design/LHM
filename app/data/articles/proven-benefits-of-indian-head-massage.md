Indian head massage is a traditional therapy with origins stretching back over a thousand years. It focuses on the head, scalp, neck and shoulders — the areas where most of us hold daily stress. The treatment offers a wide range of benefits that contribute to both physical and mental wellbeing, making it one of the most rewarding short-format therapies available.

## The Proven Benefits

[Indian head massage](/indian-head-massage) offers a variety of benefits that contribute to both physical and mental wellbeing:

1. **Stress relief.** It effectively alleviates stress and anxiety, helping to relax both the mind and body.
2. **Tension relief.** The massage targets tightness in the head, neck, shoulders and upper back, providing immediate relief from built-up tension.
3. **Improved mental clarity.** Many people experience enhanced focus and mental clarity following the treatment, making it beneficial for those feeling mentally fatigued.
4. **Better sleep.** The calming effects of the massage can lead to improved sleep quality, helping to combat insomnia or poor sleep patterns.
5. **Enhanced hair health.** By stimulating the scalp and improving blood circulation, Indian head massage can promote hair growth and reduce common hair-related issues.
6. **Increased energy.** The treatment can help to balance energy levels, leaving individuals feeling rejuvenated and revitalised.
7. **Enhanced circulation.** Improved blood flow throughout the head, neck and shoulders can boost overall health and a sense of holistic wellbeing.

Overall, Indian head massage is a therapeutic practice that combines both physical and mental health benefits, making it a great option for relaxation and rejuvenation.

## Contraindications for Indian Head Massage

The contraindications for Indian head massage include:

- Open wounds on the scalp
- Scabs on the scalp
- Allergies to oils used during the massage
- Skin conditions such as eczema or psoriasis affecting the areas being treated
- Recent head or neck injuries

### Medical Contraindications

Please check any condition that is currently being treated by a GP or another practitioner.

- Cardiovascular conditions (thrombosis, phlebitis, hypertension, hypotension, angina, pacemakers)
- Haemophilia
- Medical oedema
- Osteoporosis
- Arthritis
- Epilepsy
- Recent operations
- Diabetes
- Asthma
- Dysfunction of the nervous system (multiple sclerosis, Parkinson's disease, motor neuron disease)
- Trapped or pinched nerve (e.g. sciatica)
- Inflamed nerve
- Cancer
- Whiplash
- Slipped disc
- Acute rheumatism
- High or low blood pressure
- Any strong medications such as cocodamol

### Total Contraindications

- Contagious or infectious diseases, scalp infections
- Dysfunction of the nervous system (meningitis, inflammation of the brain)
- Fever
- Head lice
- Conjunctivitis
- Sycosis barbae
- Undiagnosed lumps and bumps
- Cervical spondylitis
- Anaphylaxis
- Vertigo
- Adhesive capsulitis (frozen shoulder)
- Bell's palsy
- Tinnitus
- Migraine, earache
- Recent head or neck injury
- Severe bruising in the treatment area
- Recent haemorrhage (especially brain)
- Arthritis or osteoporosis in the treatment area

It's important to consider these factors before booking a session to ensure safety and effectiveness. If you have any concerns or medical conditions, it's advisable to consult with a healthcare professional prior to your treatment.
